## Mini-Lesson 1 — Reflection vs refraction

reflection:
- light bounces off a surface
- angle of incidence = angle of reflection

refraction:
- light bends when it enters a new medium
- the bend happens because speed changes

common_confusion:
- if the ray bounces back, that is reflection
- if it passes into a new material and changes direction, that is refraction

## Mini-Lesson 2 — Ray-diagram logic

rule:
- a ray diagram is not just a drawing; it is an argument about what the light is doing

minimum expectations:
- label the object when relevant
- draw incident ray direction clearly
- show reflected or refracted path clearly
- keep the diagram consistent with the concept

common_mistakes:
- drawing random arrows with no relation to the source
- mixing mirror rules and lens rules
- giving the right vocabulary but the wrong diagram

## Mini-Lesson 3 — Evidence standards in physics

full_verification_needs:
- the question/prompt
- the source or review page when the question is concept-dependent
- the student’s diagram/work/answer
- enough reasoning to see whether the result is supported

warning:
- a correct-looking final answer with no diagram or reasoning may still be uncheckable
- this is especially true in optics

## Mini-Lesson 4 — How to explain a physics answer

strong explanation pattern:
- name the concept
- point to the visual or numerical evidence
- explain why the concept fits this problem

weak explanation pattern:
- "because that’s what light does"
- "I just know"
- repeating the answer with different words
