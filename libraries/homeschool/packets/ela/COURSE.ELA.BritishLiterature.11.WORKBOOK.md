## Day 132 Workbook Page

day_number: 132
lesson_title: Mixed Response Practice II — evidence, explanation, revision
tutoring_mode: NO_ANSWERING

instructions:
- Pick one prompt set and answer all parts.
- Keep your answers in your own words.
- Use the checklist at the bottom before turning it in.

### Prompt Set A
Read the assigned passage and answer:

1. What is the strongest mood in the passage?
2. What word choices or details create that mood?
3. Explain how those choices affect the reader’s understanding.

- claim:
- evidence:
- reasoning:

### Prompt Set B
Read the assigned passage and answer:

1. What attitude does the speaker or narrator seem to have?
2. Which detail best reveals that attitude?
3. What larger idea or theme does that attitude connect to?

response_space:
- claim:
- evidence:
- reasoning:

### Prompt Set C
Use two moments from the assigned reading and answer:

1. What shared idea appears in both moments?
2. How is that idea developed differently in each moment?
3. Which moment is more effective, and why?

response_space:
- shared idea:
- first moment:
- second moment:
- comparison:
- judgment:

## Revision Strip

revise_before_submit:
- Did I answer the actual question?
- Did I use specific evidence?
- Did I explain the evidence instead of just naming it?
- Did I avoid retelling too much?
- Does my answer sound like me?

## Short Practice Bank (optional support)

1. Rewrite this weak claim into a stronger one:
   "The author uses words to make it emotional."

2. Add one reasoning sentence after this evidence:
   "The speaker describes the room as silent and cold."

3. Cut one sentence of retelling from your answer and replace it with analysis.
