## Step 0 — Concept / Read (Silhouette + Intent)

### PAT-TOR-0001 — Torso Read Before Detail
IF the torso is a primary subject  
THEN its tilt, twist, and weight must read in thumbnail silhouette before anatomy detail.  
PASS: viewer can tell *lean / twist / arch* instantly.  
FAIL: “anatomy correct” but pose unclear.

### PAT-TOR-0002 — One Primary Action
IF a pose feels messy  
THEN choose one primary action for torso: **tilt OR twist OR arch** (others become secondary).  
PASS: clear hierarchy.  
FAIL: equal-intensity actions everywhere.

## Step 1 — Structure (Ribcage/Pelvis/Spine)

### PAT-TOR-0101 — Two-Mass Torso Rule
IF constructing torso  
THEN build **ribcage mass** and **pelvis mass** as separate solids connected by spine axis.  
PASS: clear separation plane.  
FAIL: single tube.

### PAT-TOR-0102 — Ribcage Has Tilt + Turn
IF ribcage rotates  
THEN indicate its **top plane** and **side plane** (at least implicitly).  
PASS: 3D turn reads without shading.  
FAIL: flat oval.

### PAT-TOR-0103 — Pelvis Is a Wedge, Not a Bowl
IF building pelvis  
THEN define its front plane and iliac crest direction as a wedge/block.  
PASS: hips have orientation.  
FAIL: “generic underwear shape.”

### PAT-TOR-0104 — Counter-Rotation Creates Torque
IF torso twists  
THEN ribcage and pelvis must counter-rotate (even slightly).  
PASS: twist feels powered.  
FAIL: upper and lower rotate same direction → weak twist.

### PAT-TOR-0105 — Spine = Compression/Extension Mechanism
IF torso bends or arches  
THEN show the spine action as compression on one side and extension on the other.  
PASS: believable bend.  
FAIL: spine drawn as straight stick.

### PAT-TOR-0106 — Center of Mass Links Torso to Support
IF figure is standing or braced  
THEN torso axis must relate to base of support (feet/seat/hand brace).  
PASS: balance looks stable.  
FAIL: floating torso.

## Step 2 — Mechanics (Forces + Landmarks + Wrap)

### PAT-TOR-0201 — Compression Side Thickens
IF torso side-bends  
THEN compressed side shortens and “stacks” forms; do not keep equal spacing.  
PASS: squash reads.  
FAIL: bend without compression.

### PAT-TOR-0202 — Stretch Side Simplifies
IF torso side-bends  
THEN stretched side uses fewer, longer form lines (avoid noisy micro-lines).  
PASS: stretch reads clean.  
FAIL: both sides equally busy.

### PAT-TOR-0203 — Abdominal Wrap Follows Twist
IF torso twists  
THEN wrapping rhythms (obliques/abdominal banding) must spiral consistently.  
PASS: twist reads via wrap.  
FAIL: vertical abs on a twisting body.

### PAT-TOR-0204 — Landmarks Anchor the Torso
IF placing anatomy masses  
THEN anchor to: sternum line, rib arc direction, navel line, iliac crest direction.  
PASS: anatomy stays stable when rotated.  
FAIL: features slide around.

### PAT-TOR-0205 — Rib Rhythm Guides Side Form
IF side torso is visible  
THEN indicate rib rhythm as an arc family (even simplified).  
PASS: side mass feels “ribbed” not flat.  
FAIL: smooth cylinder.

### PAT-TOR-0206 — Shoulder Girdle Attachment Must Be Logical
IF drawing chest/upper torso  
THEN clavicle line and shoulder socket orientation must be plausible (prevents “stuck-on arms”).  
PASS: arms feel attached.  
FAIL: arms emerge from nowhere.

### PAT-TOR-0207 — Back Plane Dominates in Back View
IF drawing back view  
THEN treat back as major plane mass before detail (lat/trap region as large shapes).  
PASS: powerful back read.  
FAIL: spaghetti muscles.

### PAT-TOR-0208 — Overlap Hierarchy for Depth
IF forms overlap (rib over pelvis; pec over deltoid)  
THEN nearer form overlaps and takes priority; far form is simplified.  
PASS: depth reads without shading.  
FAIL: equal line weight everywhere.

## Step 3 — Refinement (Anatomy as Form Language)

### PAT-TOR-0301 — Muscle Groups as Masses First
IF adding muscles  
THEN add as masses/planes, not striations.  
PASS: anatomy reads at 20% zoom.  
FAIL: detail without form.

### PAT-TOR-0302 — Chest Mass Has Direction
IF drawing pectoral/chest region  
THEN show its directional sweep and its overlap into shoulder region.  
PASS: chest “wraps.”  
FAIL: flat pec stickers.

### PAT-TOR-0303 — Abdominal Forms Obey Perspective
IF abs are visible  
THEN their spacing compresses with foreshortening and follows torso curvature.  
PASS: abs sit on a barrel.  
FAIL: evenly spaced bricks on flat torso.

### PAT-TOR-0304 — Plane Breaks Beat Contours
IF refining torso in 3/4  
THEN prioritize plane changes (front/side/top) over contour decoration.  
PASS: turn reads.  
FAIL: outline-only.

### PAT-TOR-0305 — Simplify Non-Focal Anatomy
IF torso is not focal  
THEN reduce anatomy to major planes + rhythm lines.  
PASS: composition stays readable.  
FAIL: torso steals focus.

## Step 4 — Production Standards (Skill Forge Consistency)

### PAT-TOR-0401 — Ladder Compliance
IF producing final art  
THEN preserve Step Ladder evidence:
- Step 0 thumbs
- Step 1 structure
- Step 2 masses
- Step 3 refinement
- Step 4 finish
PASS: each stage exists or is reproducible.  
FAIL: jump straight to final.

### PAT-TOR-0402 — Style Exaggeration Must Preserve Structure
IF exaggerating (heroic/manga/comic)  
THEN exaggerate *angles and volume* while preserving attachment logic and balance.  
PASS: stylized but believable.  
FAIL: broken joints/torso mechanics.
