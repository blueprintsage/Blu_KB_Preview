## PAT-ART-FIGURE-001 — Gesture First, Structure Second
IF the drawing goal is a figure (any style)  
THEN establish the gesture/line-of-action before anatomy detail.  
PASS: the pose reads in 2–3 seconds at thumbnail size.  
FAIL: stiff pose; “parts assembled” feeling.  
Evidence: Step 0 thumbs + Step 1 skeleton.

## PAT-ART-FIGURE-002 — Big Forms Before Small Forms
IF moving from Step 1 → Step 2  
THEN build ribcage + pelvis + limb cylinders (mass) before surface anatomy.  
PASS: major volumes align to gesture and weight.  
FAIL: surface detail without believable masses.

## PAT-ART-FIGURE-003 — Weight Must Land
IF the pose has contact with ground/support  
THEN show weight via: center-of-mass over base + compression on load side + stretch on free side.  
PASS: balance reads without explanation.  
FAIL: “floating” stance.

## PAT-ART-FIGURE-004 — Foreshortening Is a Value Decision
IF a form aims toward the camera  
THEN prioritize overlap + scale shift + contour wrap over “correct-looking” outlines.  
PASS: depth reads even unrendered.  
FAIL: flat shapes, equal sizing.

## PAT-ART-FIGURE-005 — Silhouette Check at Every Step
IF Step 0/1/2/3 is completed  
THEN do a silhouette read check (black-fill mental test).  
PASS: action and limbs readable.  
FAIL: tangents, limb merges, unclear action.
